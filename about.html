Short Biography

Alan Perry is a Chicago-based artist, curator, and educator working across video, installation, artist’s books, performance, and creative coding. His work traces how magic ruptures out of technology despite its alleged disenchantment. Alan received an MFA in Art & Technology Studies from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago and has taught in Chicago since 2019. His work has been exhibited and screened nationally and internationally, with recent work shown in Chicago, Illinois, Bogotá, Colombia, and Mesa, Arizona.

Long Biography

Alan Perry is a Chicago-based artist, curator, and educator working across video, installation, artist’s books, performance, and creative coding. His work traces how magic ruptures out of technology despite its alleged disenchantment. Ritual Unbinding of an Amazon Alexa integrates esoteric rituals into technical disassembly. The Gross Gloss: CD-ROM Edition is an electronic artist’s book descended from early books and multimedia, drawing on late medieval and early Renaissance treatises on magic. This project was developed from research during the Newberry Library’s Cicero Visual Artist Fellowship and has been presented at the International Congress on Medieval Studies.

Alan’s curatorial projects extend these themes. Electric Psalms brought together eight artists working with digital media and electronics who examine the relationship between mythology and technology, and Cloud-as-Ouroboros, an online pavilion for The Wrong Biennale, took the literal form of endless content scrolling to present artists using myth-making in the attention economy.

Alan received an MFA in Art & Technology Studies from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago. He has taught there and at Loyola University, University of Illinois Chicago, and Northeastern Illinois University since 2019. His teaching covers topics including holography, creative coding, and the ethical and social life of digital media. His work has been exhibited and screened across the United States and internationally, including the CICA Museum in South Korea and Voltaje in Bogotá, Colombia.
