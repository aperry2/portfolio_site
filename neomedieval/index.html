Neomedieval Cycle

This cycle of work makes use of the Medievalist subdiscipline of Neomedievalism, a deliberate and self-aware use of an invented Middle Ages. Specifically, it fully embraces the fiction that the Middle Ages were a mysterious and alien place that the modern mind would find uncomfortable to exist in. These pieces use medieval visual and architectural formats while working in Umberto Eco’s formulation of the neomedieval in “Dreaming of the Middle Ages” (1986). Just as manuscripts, altarpieces, and cathedrals constructed a medieval worldview, I use simulations, projections, and screen enclosures to surface the contemporary worldview.
